My Story

My teaching career began in Mumbai before taking me to mainland China in 2016.

My Journey

Since then, my experience has spanned kindergarten through secondary education, giving me a broad understanding of learners across developmental stages. After teaching in Shenzhen and Huzhou, I found my professional home in Zhengzhou's bilingual international schools, where I have spent the majority of my career teaching Primary Years 1–6. My work has centred on English as an Additional Language (EAL), using language as a bridge to Science, Mathematics, and cross-curricular inquiry so that multilingual learners develop both academic English and deep conceptual understanding. Alongside my classroom responsibilities, I have led Grade 5 writing moderation and mentored newly appointed teachers.

Research-informed Practice

My practitioner research for the PGCE, University of Cumbria examined how questioning strategies facilitate high-quality teaching and participation equity in bilingual classrooms. That work, grounded in Alexander's dialogic teaching framework and translanguaging theory, changed how I plan and run discussions. I now build structured wait time and open-ended questioning sequences into lessons deliberately, not as an afterthought — and I can see the difference in which students engage.

Leadership Beyond the Classroom

Beyond my own classroom, I have demonstrated the capacity to elevate instructional quality at scale. At WhiteHat Jr, I conducted quality assurance audits for a distributed team of 200+ educators, using LMS analytics to identify teaching inconsistencies and recommend targeted improvements. At GEDU Global Education, I delivered international professional development, designing and facilitating pedagogical training for trainers across campuses in the UK, Dubai, and Malta. Those roles reinforced that good teaching and good coaching require the same discipline: specific feedback, clear standards, and honest accountability.

Looking Ahead

Practitioner Research

Evidence-Based Practice

  • Master's Level
  • Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE), University of Cumbria

How do questioning strategies facilitate high-quality teaching and participation equity in bilingual classrooms?

Practitioner research examining how questioning strategies can be used to facilitate high-quality teaching and learning and support participation equity in a bilingual primary classroom.

How this research changed my classroom

This research sharpened my use of structured questioning in daily lessons. I now plan open-ended questions in advance, build in deliberate wait time, and sequence discussions so that quieter multilingual learners have multiple low-stakes entry points before whole-class sharing. The result has been broader participation — particularly among students who previously relied on stronger English speakers to answer first.

Research Journey

Module 1

Research Focus: Explored how formative feedback can strengthen writing development in multilingual primary classrooms, with a focus on improving pupil engagement, confidence, and the quality of written outcomes through evidence-informed teaching practices.

Key Professional Learning:

  • Applied evidence-informed formative feedback strategies to support writing development in multilingual classrooms.
  • Developed a deeper understanding of how timely, specific feedback can strengthen pupil confidence, independence, and engagement.
  • Used educational research to critically evaluate classroom practice and inform future teaching decisions.

Module 2

Research Focus: Investigated how supportive teacher questioning strategies, including extended wait time and follow-up prompts, can encourage greater participation, confidence, and meaningful classroom dialogue among multilingual primary learners.

  • Developed greater confidence in using extended wait time and purposeful follow-up prompts to encourage deeper pupil thinking and dialogue.

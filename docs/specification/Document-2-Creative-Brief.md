# 1. THE ONE IDEA

Every AI-built portfolio becomes an **animated résumé**: a competent, tasteful list of jobs and qualifications dressed in motion. It informs. It rarely persuades.

This portfolio will not do that. It will feel like a **beautifully typeset journal profile of a real educator** — calm, credible, human — that earns a recruiter's trust in the first five seconds and sustains it for the full scroll.

The single job of the entire site: *increase the probability that an international-school recruiter thinks — "This is exactly the kind of educator we want to interview."* (Document 1C §14.) Every decision, cut, and animation is measured against that sentence.

---

# 2. BRAND POSITIONING

Mo is not positioned as "a teacher." He is positioned at the intersection of three roles that international schools actively recruit for and rarely find together:

1. **Experienced Educator** — a decade across India and China, fluent in multilingual/bilingual primary classrooms, calm and credible.
2. **Technology-Integrated Practitioner** — a B.Sc. physicist who brings inquiry-based, cross-curricular STEM into primary/EAL practice; digitally literate without being a gimmick.
3. **Future Instructional Leader** — proven teacher-development and instructional-quality experience (mentoring trainers, assuring quality across 200+ educators), clearly growing toward curriculum/pastoral/STEM-leadership roles.

**Positioning statement (internal — not a site headline):**
*"A reflective international primary educator with a decade across two continents, who pairs classroom depth with evidence-informed STEM and teacher development — and is clearly on a trajectory into instructional leadership."*

**What this positioning is NOT:**
- Not "passionate ESL teacher" (generic, low-status).
- Not "edtech evangelist" (gimmicky, untrustworthy to traditional schools).
- Not "everything I've ever done" (overload kills the signal).

These three roles map directly to the five Portfolio Pillars in `PRD.md` (Educator, Leader, Researcher, Lifelong Learner, Global Professional). Every section must reinforce **at least one** pillar — `VISUAL_IDENTITY.md` §1 mandates this; Document 1C §5 (Multi-Role Review Board) enforces it.

# 3. RECRUITER PSYCHOLOGY

## 3.1 The primary reader
An international-school principal or head of school, scanning 40+ applications, ~60 seconds per candidate, hiring for trust and fit. Secondary readers: academic directors (pedagogical depth), HR (credentials/logistics), peers (referral), and Mo himself (long-term brand). **Design for the primary reader at all times.** Motion is forgiven when it clarifies; it is punished when it delays the answer to *who is this and can they do the job?*

## 3.2 The first 60 seconds — a silent triage
| Time | Recruiter's silent question | What the site must deliver |
|---|---|---|
| 0–5s | "Is this a real person?" | A warm, confident portrait + clear name/role/setting. No logo-spinner, no abstract hero art. *If the photo looks like stock or AI, trust dies here.* |
| 5–15s | "What's the one thing that makes him different?" | One crisp value proposition (≤14 words) + one proof of scale. One differentiator absorbed, not ten. |
| 15–30s | "Is there proof, or just claims?" | Sourced evidence of classroom impact. Unsourced claims read as marketing and erode credibility (`CONTENT_BLUEPRINT.md` rule 1). |
| 30–45s | "How does he think? Can he lead?" | Philosophy, journey, leadership — depth and trajectory, not just competence. |
| 45–60s | "Can I act?" | A persistent, low-friction path to the CV and contact. Near-zero cost to proceed. |

## 3.3 The emotional throughline
**Trust → Respect → Credibility → Intellectual regard → Confidence in the future → Ease of action.**
Every section serves one beat. Anything that interrupts the throughline is removed.

# 4. STORYTELLING & NARRATIVE ARC

The site is a single-page **cinematic narrative**, not a collection of unrelated sections (Document 1C §15; `INFORMATION_ARCHITECTURE.md` §0). Six acts give Claude a clear arc aligned to the recruiter journey — impression → journey → evidence → classroom → future → invitation, with zero backtracking.

| Act | Section(s) | Recruiter's state on entry | The act's job |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I — First Impression** | Hero | Suspicious, scanning | Earn trust in 5s: real face, real name, real role. |
| **II — The Journey** | Story, Journey | Warming, curious | Show a real human who crossed cultures and grew. |
| **III — The Evidence** | Impact, Credentials | Evaluating, proof-seeking | Replace claims with sourced proof. |
| **IV — The Classroom** | Philosophy, Moments, Research | Respecting, engaged | Show depth of thinking + genuine connection to learners. |
| **V — The Future** | Leadership | Aspirational, forward-looking | Show readiness for more responsibility, clear direction. |
| **VI — The Invitation** | Contact | Decisive, ready to act | Make the next step effortless. |

|---|---|---|
| Hero | Calm, immediate trust | Warm real portrait; name/role; one differentiator; CV + contact one tap away. |

# 5. VISUAL IDENTITY

The canonical tokens live in `VISUAL_IDENTITY.md`. This brief sets the **emotional intent** those tokens must serve. (Hexes/typography specs are authoritative there; summarized here for creative context.)

**Mood:** Editorial, not promotional. A beautifully typeset journal profile. Dark quiet backdrop, generous space. Serious, accomplished, human. *Backgrounds must never compete with content* — if noticeable while reading, too strong.

**Palette (intent):** Near-black quiet base (`--bg #0B0B0D`) with elevated surfaces for alternation. One accent — violet (`--accent #7C5CFC`) — rationed to mean *"this matters or is interactive."* Depth via surface lightness, not drop shadows. Grain ≤3%, radial washes ≤10% opacity, max one per viewport.

**Typography (intent):** Fraunces (serif, warm, academic, timeless) carries hierarchy; Manrope (readable, distinctive) carries body. Type does the heavy lifting; color and motion do little. Max 4 weights, no Inter/Roboto/Arial. Generous measure (≤68ch), calm line-height (1.7). Uppercase letter-spaced labels for eyebrows and pillar tags.

**Spacing (intent):** 8px base scale; sections 128px desktop / 80px mobile. **Whitespace is the primary hierarchy tool** — more than boxes or borders.

**Photography (intent):** Authentic, warm, natural color — no filters, duotone, or violet tinting. Portraits show *approachability and confidence over executive authority* (warmth over polish). Caption + alt on every image. Safeguarding rules apply without exception.

**Voice & tone (intent):** Reflective, precise, evidence-based, human, concise. First person, active voice, claims followed by evidence. No corporate jargon, inspirational clichés, or AI-sounding prose. No unsupported adjectives ("passionate," "dynamic," "results-driven").

**The litmus test for any visual decision:** *Does this make the recruiter trust Mo, or admire the designer?* If the latter, it is the wrong decision.

# 6. MOTION PHILOSOPHY

Motion is a servant, never a feature. The governing rules (Document 1C §9 Performance Before Beauty, §8 Remove It Test; `VISUAL_IDENTITY.md` §15):

- **Motion clarifies or it is removed.** If an animation does not aid comprehension or emotional pacing, cut it.
- **Entrances only, quiet and confident.** Fade + 8–12px rise, ease-out `cubic-bezier(0.16,1,0.3,1)`, 60ms stagger, fire once at ~20% intersection. No loops except ≤3% background drift.
- **Durations:** 150 / 300 / 500ms. Snappy, never lazy.
- **Reduced motion is mandatory.** Under `prefers-reduced-motion`, everything is instant and fully visible. No exceptions.
- **JS failure is safe.** Content is visible by default; animation only enhances. Never hide content behind a script that might not run.
- **Performance before beauty.** Reject any animation that causes measurable degradation without equivalent storytelling value (Document 1C §9).
- **No spectacle patterns:** no scroll-jacking, cursor effects, neon meshes, marquees, or parallax theatrics.

If we removed half the animations, the section should become *stronger*, not weaker.

---

# 7. 3D PHILOSOPHY

3D is opt-in and exceptional, not a default layer (Document 1C §7; stack constraints in memory).

- **Never introduce 3D for decoration.** No spinning cubes, no random particle fields, no hero orb just because WebGL is available.
- **Use 3D only where it strengthens the narrative** — e.g., a genuinely useful interactive exhibit of practice. If it's there to look impressive, it's cut.
- **Default is none.** The portfolio succeeds entirely without WebGL. Three.js is a tool for a specific storytelling need, invoked only after clearing the §7 Skill Fit Test, and only when it earns its performance and complexity cost.
- **When used:** keep it calm, purposeful, accessible (respects reduced-motion), and performant on mid-range mobile.

---

# 8. UX PRINCIPLES

- **Clarity over cleverness.** If a recruiter must decode it, it failed. Predictable, legible, fast.
- **Accessibility is non-negotiable.** WCAG AA minimum (Document 1A). Semantic HTML, valid landmarks, keyboard navigation, visible focus, descriptive slugs, screen-reader compatibility, reduced-motion support. Fully readable with JS disabled.
- **Mobile-first, China-aware.** Many recruiters are mobile and China-based. Self-hosted fonts (Google Fonts blocked in mainland China), responsive at 480/768/1024/1280, touch targets ≥44px, no hover-dependent information.
- **One dominant action per viewport.** The primary CTA (Download CV) owns the viewport; secondary actions stay quiet (`VISUAL_IDENTITY.md` §11).
- **Evidence-first hierarchy.** The strongest emphasis is reserved for evidence, not decoration (`VISUAL_IDENTITY.md` §1). Proof outranks polish.
- **Calm authority.** Composed, precise, warm. Nothing shouts. Navigation effortless; interactions predictable.
- **Forgiveness.** Graceful fallbacks for missing user assets (image placeholders, poster instead of missing video) so the page never shows broken chrome.

# 9. SUCCESS METRICS

**Technical (Lighthouse, mobile, throttled — `PRD.md` §4):**
- Performance ≥ 90 at launch (≥ 95 post-optimization)
- Accessibility 100
- Best Practices 100
- SEO 100
- FCP < 1.5s

**Recruiter-journey outcomes (the real scoreboard):**
- 0–5s: understands who Mo is (Hero) without scrolling.
- 5–15s: absorbs one differentiator + value proposition.
- 15–30s: sees one concrete sourced example of impact.
- 30–45s: confirms depth (philosophy, leadership, journey).
- 45–60s: downloads CV or makes contact.

**Acceptance (qualitative):** A recruiter, without scrolling past the first two screens, can answer — *Who is this? What impact has he made? Can he teach and lead? Is there evidence? How do I contact him / get his CV?* If any answer is unclear, the section is not done.

**Longevity:** Still looks modern in five years (Document 1C §10). Would Apple / Stripe / Linear / Vercel ship it? If not, improve it.

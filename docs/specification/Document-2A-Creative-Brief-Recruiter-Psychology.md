# 1. PURPOSE

Project Meridian is not a website.

It is a carefully engineered professional experience whose purpose is to increase recruiter confidence and ultimately generate interview invitations.

Everything that follows exists to support one measurable outcome:

> **A recruiter should reach the end of the portfolio convinced that Mohammed Shehzad Khan deserves an interview.**

Every animation.

Every image.

Every word.

Every transition.

Every interaction.

Every visual hierarchy decision.

Every colour.

Every piece of whitespace.

Everything must contribute to that objective.

---

# 2. THE BIG IDEA

The portfolio shall communicate one central message:

> **"An experienced international educator who combines outstanding classroom practice with modern educational technology, reflective leadership and continuous professional growth."**

Everything else supports this statement.

If something does not reinforce this message,

it should be removed.

# 4. WHAT RECRUITERS REALLY BUY

Recruiters are not buying qualifications.

They are buying confidence.

Confidence that this person will:

* improve learning
* inspire children
* collaborate with colleagues
* represent the school well
* remain professional
* adapt quickly
* embrace innovation

The portfolio must sell confidence,

not credentials.

# 5. RECRUITER PSYCHOLOGY

# 19. WHAT TO AVOID

Claude Code shall avoid:

* Generic portfolio templates
* Overused education icons
* Cartoon classroom graphics
* Excessive gradients
* Random floating particles
* Constant motion
* Auto-playing audio
* Long walls of text
* Infinite scrolling without structure
* Decorative animations with no purpose
* Excessive 3D objects
* Heavy GPU effects that slow navigation
* Trend-driven gimmicks that will age quickly

If an effect exists only because it "looks cool," it should be questioned before implementation.

---

# 20. CREATIVE NORTH STAR

If the recruiter closes the portfolio after two minutes, the lasting impression should be:

> "This educator is experienced, thoughtful, technologically capable, internationally minded, and someone I would be confident placing in front of students, parents, and colleagues."

Everything in Project Meridian should move the visitor toward that conclusion.

---

## Creative Director's Critique

This is a strong foundation, but I'd make one significant refinement before Claude starts designing.

The portfolio should not try to impress recruiters with technology.

It should **use technology to quietly reinforce credibility**.

The most memorable educational portfolios are not those with the most animations—they are those where every interaction feels intentional and effortless. The technology disappears into the experience, allowing the educator's story and evidence to remain the focus.

That principle should guide every creative decision going forward. It aligns with your goal of building a premium, recruiter-focused portfolio that is visually exceptional without sacrificing clarity, accessibility, or professionalism.

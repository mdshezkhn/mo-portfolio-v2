## Mission
> **This portfolio exists to reduce hiring risk by providing authentic, verifiable evidence of professional competence, character, and potential contribution.**

## Recruiter Decision Framework
| Recruiters' Hidden Question | Evidence That Answers It |
|-----------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Can they teach?** | Classroom artifacts, student work, assessment trends, lesson video |
| **Will they fit our school?** | Philosophy, collaboration, safeguarding, cultural experience |
| **Can we trust them?** | Qualifications, references, timeline consistency, research, endorsements |
| **Will they stay?** | Career progression, commitment, location, availability |
| **Will parents trust them?** | Professional communication, classroom environment, testimonials, presentation |
| **Can they contribute beyond their classroom?** | Teacher mentoring, curriculum design, moderation, professional development, leadership projects |

> **Every section must reduce at least one recruiter uncertainty through the strongest available evidence.**

## The 30‑Second Scan Test
Give the portfolio to an unfamiliar reviewer. Exactly **30 seconds**. Ask:

1. What role is this teacher seeking?
2. What are they unusually good at?
3. What evidence did you notice?
4. Would you interview them? Why or why not?
5. What do you still not know?
6. **What concerns would stop you interviewing this candidate?**

> **Repeat with at least three independent reviewers.** If answers to #1–3 are unclear, the homepage fails.

## Governance Rules
### Integrity
| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Consistency Rule** | Professional identity (roles, dates, certifications, etc.) must remain consistent across all sections. |
| **Single Source of Truth Rule** | Professional facts originate from one authoritative master record (CV/master career document). **All public‑facing professional profiles must align.** |
| **Authenticity Rule** | Evidence must refrain from exaggerating scale, certainty, or impact. Use conservative wording. |

### Evidence
| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Proportionality Rule** | Evidence strength must match claim significance. Extraordinary claims require robust proof (e.g., citations, anonymized metrics). |
| **Continuous Evidence Rule** | Within the same evidence category, stronger evidence should replace weaker evidence rather than simply accumulate alongside it. The objective is continual improvement in evidence quality, not continual growth in content volume. |
| **Evidence Freshness Rule** | Evidence should remain representative of current professional practice. Older evidence may be retained where it demonstrates enduring competence or leadership, but obsolete or unrepresentative evidence should be replaced. |
| **Evidence Ethics Rule** | Never present implied causation, inflated metrics, or unverifiable claims. Where outcomes have multiple contributing factors, describe the candidate's contribution accurately and proportionately. |

### Communication
| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Recruiter Cognitive Load Rule** | Every screen must communicate one primary idea; the primary message must be clear before supporting detail. |
| **Signal‑to‑Noise Rule** | Every addition must increase the ratio of verifiable evidence to promotional language. Strengthen credibility; reduce clutter. |
| **Deletion Rule** | When unsure whether to retain content: *If removing it does not reduce recruiter confidence, remove it.* |
| **Decision Test** | Before adding, removing, or modifying any element, answer:
1. Which recruiter question does this change address?
2. What evidence supports making this change?
3. How will success be evaluated?
If these questions cannot be answered, the change should not proceed. |

### Governance
| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Audience Rule** | The primary audience for this portfolio is international‑school recruiters, school leaders, and hiring decision‑makers. Feedback from other audiences may inform future improvements but must not override evidence gathered from the intended hiring audience. |
| **Versioning Rule** | Minor evidence updates (new work samples, testimonials, etc.) do **not** require a new version. Governance/structural changes require a new version (1.1+) with documented rationale. |

## Maintenance Cycle
**Routine Updates (Quarterly)**  
• Classroom artefacts  
• Publications  
• Professional development  
• Testimonials  

**Emergency Updates**  
• Major qualification  
• Employment change  
• Research publication  
• Teaching award  
• Safeguarding certification  

**Structural Review (Annual)**  
Structural review is permitted only when recruiter feedback, analytics, or hiring outcomes demonstrate a meaningful problem.

> Historical evidence should not be removed unless it becomes inaccurate or is replaced by stronger evidence.  
> **Cosmetic changes should not be made unless they demonstrably improve readability, accessibility, usability, or recruiter comprehension.**

---

## Closing Statement
> **Frozen Purpose**  
> This portfolio is a professional recruitment asset, not a design project.  
> Its purpose: *Reduce hiring risk through authentic evidence of classroom practice, leadership, research, and growth.*  
> Governance: Future changes must reduce hiring risk or strengthen evidence.  
> Evolution: Driven by stronger evidence and validated recruiter outcomes, not visual trends or personal preference.  
> **Success is measured by qualified interview invitations, shortlisting rate, and offer rate.**  
> **In the event of conflict between design preference and recruiter evidence, recruiter evidence takes precedence.**  
> **Version 1.0 establishes the governing principles for future development. All revisions require documented, evidence‑driven justification.**

# Gallery Evidence Plan: Curation Guide

The gallery ("Moments in Pedagogy") is not a photo album; it is a visual evidence locker. Every image must serve a specific psychological purpose in convincing a Head of School that you are a highly capable international educator.

Do not upload generic photos of smiling children looking at the camera. Upload photos that demonstrate pedagogy in action.

## Recommended Assets

### Image 1: Dialogic Teaching
- **Purpose:** Active questioning and student discussion.
- **Visual:** The teacher leaning in or prompting, while a student is mid-sentence, perhaps gesturing to the board or a peer.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They use structured wait time and encourage student-led discourse."

### Image 2: STEM Integration
- **Purpose:** Hands-on science or math application.
- **Visual:** Students actively manipulating materials (e.g., building a circuit, measuring liquids) while the teacher observes or facilitates.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They know how to move beyond textbook theory into practical, inquiry-based STEM."

### Image 3: Literacy / EAL Scaffolding
- **Purpose:** Demonstrating support for multilingual learners.
- **Visual:** A close-up or over-the-shoulder shot of a student using a bilingual graphic organizer, word wall, or sentence frame.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They actively scaffold language acquisition."

### Image 4: Formative Assessment
- **Purpose:** Giving immediate, targeted feedback.
- **Visual:** The teacher crouching next to a student's desk, pointing to a specific part of their written work, engaged in a 1-on-1 conference.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They practice Assessment for Learning (AfL) in the moment."

### Image 5: Collaborative Learning
- **Purpose:** Group work dynamics.
- **Visual:** A small group of students working together on a single artifact (a poster, a puzzle, a laptop), demonstrating peer-to-peer collaboration.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They can manage dynamic, multi-directional classroom activities."

### Image 6: Teacher Workshop / Leadership
- **Purpose:** Demonstrating capacity beyond the classroom.
- **Visual:** The candidate standing in front of peers, pointing to a slide, or facilitating a round-table discussion with other adult educators.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They are a leader and a resource for the wider faculty."

### Image 7: The Learning Environment
- **Purpose:** Showcasing classroom management and setup.
- **Visual:** A wide shot of the classroom showing organized desks, student work on the walls (not store-bought posters), and clear routines.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They maintain a safe, organized, and student-centered learning environment."

### Image 8: Technology Integration
- **Purpose:** Modern tool usage.
- **Visual:** Students appropriately using iPads or Chromebooks for a specific task (e.g., recording a Seesaw response), rather than passive screen time.
- **Recruiter Takeaway:** "They integrate technology purposefully."

---

## Technical & Professional Guidelines

1. **Anonymity & Privacy:** Ensure you have the right to use these images. If in doubt, use photos where student faces are obscured (shot from behind), blurred slightly, or crop the image to focus on the hands/work.
2. **Lighting & Quality:** Photos should be well-lit (natural daylight is best). Blurry or excessively dark photos damage professional credibility.
3. **Format:** Export all final images as **WebP** (`.webp`) to ensure the portfolio loads instantly.
4. **Composition:** Use the rule of thirds. The focus should be on the *action of learning*, not the teacher posing.

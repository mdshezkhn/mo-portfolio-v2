## Research Question
How do questioning strategies facilitate high-quality teaching and participation equity
in bilingual primary classrooms?

## Context
Bilingual primary classrooms often show unequal participation: stronger-English speakers
answer first, while multilingual learners with valuable ideas stay silent. This practitioner
research investigated whether structured questioning could broaden who takes part.
Theoretical framing: Alexander's *dialogic teaching* and *translanguaging* theory.

## Method
- **Approach:** Practitioner inquiry across two PGCE modules (PGCE7002).
- **Module 1:** formative feedback and writing development in multilingual classrooms.
- **Module 2:** questioning strategies, extended wait time, and follow-up prompts.
- **Data:** classroom evidence (participation patterns, pupil responses) analysed via
  thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke), within BERA ethical guidelines.

## Classroom Implications
Structured wait time and open-ended questioning sequences are now planned deliberately,
not as an afterthought. Quieter multilingual learners get multiple low-stakes entry points
before whole-class sharing — broadening participation beyond the first-responders.

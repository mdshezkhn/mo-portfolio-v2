About Me

My teaching career began in Mumbai and moved to mainland China in 2016. Since then, I have spent the better part of a decade in Zhengzhou's bilingual international schools — teaching EAL and English across primary levels, leading Grade 5 writing moderation, and mentoring teachers joining the department. That sustained experience in a single complex context has given me something harder to acquire than credentials: fluency in the specific classroom dynamics of multilingual primary learners.

My practitioner research for the Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE), University of Cumbria examined how questioning strategies facilitate high-quality teaching and participation equity in bilingual classrooms. That work, grounded in Alexander's dialogic teaching framework and translanguaging theory, changed how I plan and run discussions. I now build structured wait time and open-ended questioning sequences into lessons deliberately, not as an afterthought — and I can see the difference in which students engage.

Beyond my own classroom, I have demonstrated the capacity to elevate instructional quality at scale. At WhiteHat Jr / Byju's, I contributed to quality assurance for a distributed team of 200+ educators, using LMS analytics to identify teaching inconsistencies. At GEDU Global Education, I supported international professional development, delivering pedagogical training for trainers across campuses in the UK, Dubai, and Malta. Those roles reinforced that good teaching and good coaching require the same discipline: specific feedback, clear standards, and honest accountability.

I hold a Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE), University of Cumbria, a B.Ed (University of Kashmir), an M.A. in English Language and Literature, and a B.Sc. in Physics. The science degree is not incidental — it directly supports the cross-curricular STEM integration work I bring to primary classrooms. I am seeking an August 2027 position in an international or UK-aligned school in the Gulf or Southeast Asia where I can grow into curriculum or instructional leadership roles.

Practitioner Research

Evidence-Based Practice

  • Master's Level
  • Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE), University of Cumbria

How do questioning strategies facilitate high-quality teaching and participation equity in bilingual classrooms?

Practitioner research examining how questioning strategies can be used to facilitate high-quality teaching and learning and support participation equity in a bilingual primary classroom.

How this research changed my classroom

This research sharpened my use of structured questioning in daily lessons. I now plan open-ended questions in advance, build in deliberate wait time, and sequence discussions so that quieter multilingual learners have multiple low-stakes entry points before whole-class sharing. The result has been broader participation — particularly among students who previously relied on stronger English speakers to answer first.
